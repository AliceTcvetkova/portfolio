Institute for the Development of Vocational Education (IRPO)

Professionalitet Platform

Context

At the federal level, there was no unified digital system for managing data on educational programs, educational organizations, and educational standards.

Information existed in fragmented form:

  • Regulatory documents
  • Educational standards
  • Local organizational processes
  • Various data storage formats

Scaling the Professionalitet program required a single information platform.

Problem

Core business processes across educational organizations varied significantly.

Key data were spread across hundreds of documents and regulatory acts.

There was no unified data structure that would enable:

  • Process automation
  • Information reuse
  • Consistent requirements nationwide

My Role

I was responsible for domain research, system requirements, data structure design, and coordinating product development from concept through pilot launch.

Approach

User Research

I conducted more than 100 interviews with representatives of four user segments.

I studied:

  • Current workflows
  • User pain points
  • Legislative requirements
  • Constraints of the education sector

Regulatory Analysis

I analyzed more than 400 educational standards and regulatory documents.

I identified:

  • Key entities
  • Relationships between them
  • Mandatory business processes
  • Data storage requirements

System Design

Based on the research, I:

  • Defined a unified industry domain model
  • Documented business processes
  • Designed the data structure
  • Laid the foundation for the system's first centralized database

I also initiated automation of data collection and upload workflows into the system.

Development Management

I coordinated the contractor's work:

  • Task definition
  • Implementation oversight
  • Testing
  • Compliance with legal requirements
  • Acceptance of deliverables

Solution

A unified information system was built and launched, bringing previously fragmented educational processes together on a single digital platform.

The system established a consistent approach to storing, processing, and using data within the Professionalitet program.

Results

  • Conducted more than 100 user interviews.
  • Analyzed more than 400 educational standards.
  • Created the system's first centralized data model.
  • Unified key educational processes.
  • Launched the platform from concept to pilot in under one year.
  • Built the foundation for further digitization of vocational education.

Digital Educational Content Ecosystem

Context

After establishing a unified data structure, the next challenge was to enable large-scale production of high-quality digital educational content and its reuse across educational organizations.

Problem

Content was created in isolation.

Each team worked by its own rules.

This led to:

  • Duplicated work
  • Inconsistent material quality
  • Scaling difficulties
  • Ineffective content reuse

My Role

I was responsible for building a content production methodology, rolling out processes at the federal level, and developing the information system for storing and scaling educational materials.

Approach

Methodology Design

I designed a unified workflow for producing digital educational content.

The methodology covered:

  • Production processes
  • Participant roles
  • Quality requirements
  • Content structure
  • Publishing rules

Scaling

I organized rollout of the methodology to more than 130 production teams.

This included:

  • Training
  • Presentations
  • Implementation support
  • Consulting

Platform Development

Building on the previously created data model, I initiated development of a dedicated information system for storing and reusing educational content.

The system linked materials to educational standards and enabled reuse across disciplines and programs.

Solution

A unified methodology for producing digital educational content was implemented, along with a centralized information system for storage and distribution.

Results

  • Scaled a unified methodology to 130+ teams nationwide.
  • Produced more than 2,000 units of digital educational content in the first year.
  • Launched a new information system in under three months.
  • Enabled reuse of educational materials across organizations and disciplines.
  • Built a unified digital knowledge base for vocational education.
  • Established infrastructure for standardization and quality improvement of educational content at the federal level.
