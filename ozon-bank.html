Ozon Bank

Early Payments

Context

Early Payments is a new Ozon Bank financial product designed to accelerate access to funds for clients before standard payout dates. The project touched a large number of internal systems and required synchronization across teams from different domains.

Problem

Launching the new product required aligning a large number of dependent systems and teams. During planning, there was a risk of missing the release deadline due to high integration complexity and insufficient readiness in parts of the development work.

My Role

I was responsible for coordinating the cross-domain product launch, managing dependencies between teams, and overseeing execution of the release plan.

Approach

  • Coordinated work across more than 20 teams.
  • Led planning and tracked task execution across the full project scope.
  • Managed risks and dependencies between workstreams.
  • Organized regular project syncs and a transparent status-tracking system.
  • Analyzed the impact of changes on launch timelines and critical paths.

Solution

During implementation, a risk emerged of delaying the release by approximately four weeks. After analyzing dependencies, I initiated shifting part of the development work and rebuilding the delivery plan.

I also established cross-team collaboration and execution-control processes, which reduced organizational risk and ensured stable preparation for launch.

Results

  • Launched a new financial product.
  • Release delivered on the planned timeline.
  • Avoided a potential ~4-week launch delay.
  • More than 20 teams involved in the project.
  • Release completed without critical incidents.
  • Delivery scope expanded from the initial MVP to a more mature product version.

What Was the Hardest Part

The hardest challenge was managing dependencies across more than 20 teams while balancing launch deadlines against the scope of functionality being delivered.

Classic Factoring

Context

Classic factoring is a financial product for financing suppliers against future payouts. It was an active business line with significant transaction volume and required ongoing development and support.

Problem

Scaling the product required expanding functionality, maintaining coordination between internal services and contractors, and reducing the cost of ownership for individual components of the solution.

My Role

I was responsible for product development, coordinating engineering work, and managing collaboration between internal teams and external contractors.

Approach

  • Managed product development with turnover exceeding $1M.
  • Coordinated internal teams and external contractors.
  • Organized migration and adaptation of solutions between services.
  • Participated in launching new product capabilities.
  • Analyzed architectural and economic constraints of existing solutions.

Solution

During product development, I analyzed one of the solutions delivered by a contractor. Based on the analysis, a new implementation was prepared that allowed us to move away from part of the existing workflow and optimize costs.

I also ensured migration of functionality between services and the launch of new capabilities for product users.

Results

  • Sustained development of a product with turnover exceeding $1M.
  • Successfully coordinated internal and external development stakeholders.
  • Launched multiple new product capabilities.
  • Redesigned a contractor-delivered solution.
  • Economic impact of at least $60,000 per month.
  • Improved solution efficiency and reduced operating costs.

What Was the Hardest Part

The main challenge was evolving a financial product with many integrations and contractors while simultaneously finding opportunities for substantial operational cost reduction.
