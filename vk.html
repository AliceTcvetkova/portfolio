VK · Digital Products

Product Analytics & Development Management Systems

Context

At VK, I worked on product and analytics systems used to drive product development decisions and engineering management. The work spanned multiple teams and products that required a unified approach to data, processes, and architecture.

Problem

Product and engineering data were spread across different systems and teams.

This led to several issues:

  • No single source of reliable information
  • Metric discrepancies between teams
  • Duplicated work
  • Harder decision-making
  • Extra cost for development and maintenance

There was also no unified process for managing changes and tracking task delivery across development stakeholders.

My Role

I was responsible for evolving internal product and analytics systems, coordinating cross-functional teams, and organizing change-delivery processes.

Approach

Data & Process Discovery

I analyzed product, system, and organizational data to understand how decisions were made and where the main efficiency losses occurred.

As a result, we identified:

  • Gaps in data structure
  • Duplicated functionality across teams
  • Misaligned development processes
  • Lack of transparency in initiative implementation status

Cross-Team Collaboration

I organized collaboration between:

  • Architects
  • Developers
  • Analysts
  • Product teams

We aligned on a shared understanding of data structure and system requirements.

Process Design

I introduced a more structured approach to engineering management:

  • Sprint planning
  • Delivery tracking
  • Dependency management
  • Regular project syncs

Solution

I initiated a unified product data structure and processes for consolidating information across multiple systems.

We launched data collection and aggregation workflows that formed a single source of truth for product decision-making.

We also identified and documented cases of duplicated development across teams, which allowed us to rethink task allocation and the evolution of internal services.

Results

  • Established a unified approach to product and system information.
  • Built a single source of truth for multiple products.
  • Reduced inconsistencies between teams.
  • Increased transparency of development processes and implementation status.
  • Surfaced duplicated development processes across teams.
  • Potential economic impact from addressing identified inefficiencies estimated at ~20M RUB per year.
  • Improved predictability of change delivery and decision quality.
